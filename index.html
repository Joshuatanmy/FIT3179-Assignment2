The prevelance of Heart Disease Globally

The global obesity epidemic is well established, with increases in obesity prevalence for most countries since the 1980s. Obesity contributes directly to incident cardiovascular risk factors, including dyslipidemia, type 2 diabetes, hypertension, and sleep disorders.

Burning calories is a product of how long you exercise

It's been established that obesity is a link to diabetes, so to reduce the risk of diabetes, it makes sense to lose fat. A common method of doing so is to burn more calories than you eat. The visualisation demonstrates the correlation between the duration you spend in the gym, and the amount of calories you burn as a result.


The type of exercise can also affect the number of calories you burn while exercising, so it's worth it to investigate which exercise might be more suitable for certain people. And the data also reveals an interesting relationship between the exercise duration of an individual And how frequently they will exercise.
